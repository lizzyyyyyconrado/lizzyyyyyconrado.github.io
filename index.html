King and his Backgrounds

Martin Luther King Jr. was such an American Baptist minister and activist who became the most visible spokesperson and leader in the civil rights movement from 1955 until 1968. King is most known for the words he gave during civil rights protest, without violence and civil disobedience. King led the bus boycott in 1955 and two years later in 1997, became the first president of the Southern Christian Leadership Conferenece. His famous "I Have a Dream" speech was organized in the 1963 March on Washington. On October 14, 1964, Martin Luther King Jr, won the Nobel Peace Prize for nonviolent resistance. In 1968, he was planning a national occupation in Washington D.C, when he was killed on April 4 in Tennessee. His death leads to riots in U.S. cities. The man who killed Martin Luther King Jr, was sentenced to 99 years in prison and he was 41 at the time. He was able to serve 29 years, before he died from hepatitis in 1998. King was also awarded the President Medal of Freedom and the Congressional Gold Medal.
